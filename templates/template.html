Privileged accounts and credentials represent one of the largest security vulnerabilities an organization faces today. In the hands of an external attacker or malicious insider, privileged accounts allow attackers to take full control of an organization's IT infrastructure, disable security controls, steal confidential information, commit financial fraud and disrupt operations. With this growing threat, organizations need to gain visibility into the accounts that enable attackers to be successful. CyberArk Discovery and Audit (DNA) provides a tool that enables organizations to gain insight into the breadth of their attack surface, risk of credential theft, and overall privileged account security health.

Overview

The following represents a high-level recap of the findings of the DNA scan, as contained in the results files provided by Customer Name. It is important to note that the results within this report only pertain to the machines that the DNA tool was able to connect to on the day of the scan. It cannot connect to machines which are powered off, disconnected from the network, or were otherwise unavailable to the tool. Therefore these results should be regarded only as a representative sample of the environment rather than a complete health check.

Hardcoded Credentials

IIS, WebSphere and WebLogic Application Accounts with Plain-text Passwords

Hard-coded credentials are particularly important because they offer an attacker an easy way to escalate privilege. Obtaining a plain-text password from an application file means that the attacker does not have to waste time and effort cracking encrypted credentials, or risk detection by attempting a brute-force attack.

Overall Account Health

Compliant and Non-compliant Accounts

Account health can be measured in terms of the age of individual passwords within the environment. Generally speaking, the older a password is the more likely it is to be stolen, and the larger the window of opportunity is for exploiting that credential. Particularly with privileged accounts it is important to implement a rotation policy that ensures that passwords are changed regularly.
